Decentralisation: The Best of Both Worlds

Centralised

Current payment systems are highly centralised, relying on third party networks like Swift to interact with each other. Highly centralised systems are very fast but they have single points of failure, you have to have complete trust in the one entity, and if they disappear, your data and money does too. Some internet payment networks have withheld funds from legal entities for no clear reason.

Distributed

This problem has been solved by totally distributed systems, like Bitcoin. However, the gain of totally trustless transactions comes at a cost of network efficiency; slow confirmation times, an ever growing blockchain, and energy intensive mining. Bitcoin can always be improved, but fully distributed networks are systemically slower than centralised networks by their very nature.

Decentralised

We believe there are trade-offs between the two and that decentralisation is the sweet spot. Small amounts of trust reap huge gains in efficiency. The vast majority of the world are used to trusting single entities, public or private, and in many cases prefer to have some sort of entity responsible. hyperledger can evolve the current system to bring the benefits of revolutionary distributed systems.

Learn More

Free

Zero cost to transfer assets inside the network. Completely free to create new assets. Completely free to deploy.

Near Instant

No long confirmation times. No waiting for ledgers to close. Transactions are confirmed in less than a second, not minutes.

Flexible

Consensus pools can have their own membership requirements. Sub-pools can be created in just one juristiction.

Open

Not controlled by any single company. Completely open source. No pre-allocated native currency built in to the protocol.

Efficient

Capable of processing tens of thousands of transactions per second without energy and computationally expensive mining.

Simple

A straightforward HTTP service that is easy to use and deploy. Integrate with all your favourite libraries and web services.

Consensus Pools

testpool

testpool is an open free for all. Try out your ledgers and third party applications here, or try and hack the system. Do whatever you want but beware, data could be wiped at any time!

mainpool

mainpool is the primary pool that is open to anyone. Nodes in this pool require a unique domain name and an SSL cert. Please join our mailing list to be notified of its launch.

*custom*pool

Since hyperledger is free and open source, anyone is welcome to create their own pools for private or public usage. Perhaps you want a pool limited to just your country for regulatory reasons, or even just your university.

premiumpool

premiumpool is our commercially supported pool. Restrictions and requirements for operating a consensus node are more stringent and claims made by ledgers are audited. For more information, please Get in Touch
